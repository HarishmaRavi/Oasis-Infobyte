MARY KOM

Mary Kom is a renowned Indian boxer and a trailblazer in women's boxing globally. Born on March 1, 1983, in Manipur, India, she has won numerous accolades including multiple World Championships and an Olympic bronze medal. Mary Kom is celebrated not only for her sporting achievements but also for breaking barriers and inspiring women athletes worldwide. Her resilience, dedication, and talent have made her a symbol of empowerment and excellence in the world of sports.

Early Life and Background

  • Mary Kom was born into a family of modest means. Her parents, Mangte Tonpa Kom and Mangte Akham Kom, worked as tenant farmers. She grew up in a small village with her siblings, experiencing the challenges and joys of rural life in Manipur
  • From a young age, Mary Kom showed an interest in athletics and sports. She was initially drawn to athletics but later discovered her passion for boxing.
  • Mary Kom's early life not only shaped her character but also laid the foundation for her remarkable journey in boxing. Her experiences growing up in Manipur and her initial struggles are integral parts of her inspiring story of perseverance and achievement.

Boxing Career

  • INTERNATIONAL DEBUT: She made her international debut in 2001 at the Women's World Boxing Championship held in the United States, where she won a silver medal. This marked the beginning of her illustrious international career.
  • WORLD CHAMPIONSHIPS: Mary Kom is a six-time World Amateur Boxing Champion, winning gold medals in 2002, 2005, 2006, 2008, 2010, and 2018. She also has several other medals from World Championships, cementing her status as one of the most successful amateur boxers globally.
  • OLYMPIC GAMES : Women's boxing was included in the Olympic Games for the first time in 2012, and Mary Kom competed in the flyweight (51 kg) category. She won a bronze medal at the London Olympics, becoming the first Indian female boxer to achieve this feat.
  • ASIAN GAMES : Mary Kom has won several medals at the Asian Games, including a gold medal in 2014 (Incheon) and a bronze medal in 2010 (Guangzhou).
  • COMMONWEALTH GAMES: She has also participated in the Commonwealth Games, winning a gold medal in 2018 (Gold Coast) in the women's flyweight category.

Beyond Boxing

  • POLITICS : Mary Kom was nominated as a Member of Parliament (MP) in the Rajya Sabha, the upper house of the Indian Parliament, in 2016.
  • ACADEMICS AND EDUCATION : Mary Kom runs the Mary Kom-SAI Boxing Academy in Manipur to train young and aspiring boxers.
  • PHILANTHROPY: She actively participates in various charitable activities and social causes, particularly focusing on the empowerment of women and children.
  • PUBLIC SPEAKING AND MOTIVATION: Mary Kom often delivers motivational speeches and participates in events to inspire young athletes and individuals.
  • FLIM AND MEDIA : Her life and achievements inspired a Bollywood biographical film titled "Mary Kom," released in 2014, starring Priyanka Chopra.
